About

I like understanding the system behind the feature.

I am particularly motivated by the structural and analytical side of software engineering: how components interact, how requirements influence design and how technical decisions affect a product over time.

My work spans distributed systems, compiler optimization, full-stack development, WebGL graphics and object-storage architecture. The common thread is systems thinking: making responsibilities clear, connecting technologies coherently and reducing unnecessary complexity.

I care about maintainability and quality, while recognizing that professional engineering also requires pragmatic decisions, incremental improvement and delivery within real constraints.

Approach

How I think about engineering work.

A few principles that guide how I understand problems, structure systems and improve software.

01

Understand the context

Start with the problem, users, constraints and risks before selecting technology.

02

Make boundaries clear

Explicit responsibilities and interfaces make systems easier to maintain and debug.

03

Make behaviour visible

Trace data and requests across important boundaries to progressively narrow problems.

04

Balance quality and delivery

Apply testing, automation and process in proportion to the system and its real needs.
